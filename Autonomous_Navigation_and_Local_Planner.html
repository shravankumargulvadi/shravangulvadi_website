Overview:

As part of a hands-on exploration in robotics, I successfully assembled a JetRacer robot and advanced it into a sophisticated autonomous navigator. This project involved mechanical assembly, integration of ROS navigation stack and development of a custom State Lattice based local planner for the robot.

Autonomous Navigation and Mapping:

The video shows the Monte-Carlo Localization and Mapping algorithm in action. The algorithm uses the output of the RP lidar and the onboard IMU to localize the robot and map the environment. This task showcased the robot's ability to understand and interact with its environment autonomously. A key task in the project was the development of the state-lattice based local planner using the kinematic bicycle model of the JetRacer. This custom planner, crucial for the robot's decision-making, works in conjunction with the A* global planner. The A* planner utilizes the mapped environment to autonomously navigate the robot to its goal position. While the local planner helps the robot avoid any dynamic obstacles while reaching the waypoints of the global planner.
