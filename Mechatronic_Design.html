  • Objective: The challenge was to design a High Precision Rotary assembly from Scratch to Production-ready in three days.
  • Design process:

    The workflow began with the critical task of estimating torque requirements, which formed the basis for selecting an appropriate motor and gearbox. The goal was to meet the performance demands of the application with precision.

    Following the selection, the output torque was calculated to confirm its adequacy. If found inadequate, the starting torque and friction torque were updated to optimize the system.

    Attention was then given to mechanical integration, focusing on selecting a gearbox in line with the shaft diameter, leading to the meticulous design of the shaft itself.

    Subsequently, bearings were chosen to support the shaft and facilitate smooth rotation, a key factor in reducing friction and enhancing the assembly's longevity.

    The final stages included the design of custom parts, system integration, and a comprehensive design validation process. This ensured that the assembly performed flawlessly under real-world conditions.

    Throughout this process, any issues encountered led to a revisitation of earlier steps, enabling iterative refinements. This dynamic and flexible approach was pivotal in achieving a robust design within a constrained three-day period.
