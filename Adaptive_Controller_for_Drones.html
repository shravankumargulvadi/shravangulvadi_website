Overview:

In the realm of aerial robotics, ensuring stability and safety, especially during unforeseen circumstances, is paramount. Our latest project embodies this commitment to reliability and innovation. We have meticulously designed an adaptive controller specifically for the DJI Mavic drone, aiming to address and stabilize the drone during critical rotor malfunctions mid-flight.

The Challenge:

Rotor malfunctions in drones can lead to catastrophic outcomes, particularly in crucial applications like aerial photography, surveillance, or delivery systems. Recognizing this, our team set out to develop a solution that not only addresses this issue but does so in a way that is seamless and efficient.

Our Solution:

The core of our solution lies in the Model Reference Adaptive Controller (MRAC). This cutting-edge controller dynamically adjusts to varying flight conditions, specifically focusing on scenarios where a rotor malfunction leads to significant thrust loss. By continuously comparing the drone's performance to a predefined optimal flight model, MRAC makes real-time adjustments to ensure stable flight.

Innovative Comparison Approach:

To validate the efficacy of our solution, we conducted a comparative analysis with the Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR), a standard in drone control mechanisms. This comparison not only highlights the robustness of MRAC in handling high-stress flight scenarios but also demonstrates its superiority in maintaining stability and control.

Key Results:

The adaptive controller has proven its effectiveness in scenarios leading up to a thrust loss of 77% due to rotor malfunction. This level of resilience in maintaining flight stability is a significant advancement in drone technology.

Impact:

Our innovative approach in drone control opens new avenues for the safe and reliable use of drones in various applications. Whether it's for commercial delivery, aerial photography, or critical surveillance, our adaptive controller ensures that drones remain stable and functional, even in the most challenging conditions.
